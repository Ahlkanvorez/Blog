The Good, the Beautiful, the Simple, the Sublime, the Just — these things all transcend their examples, and when cultivated together by minds in discussion cast light on what it means to have wisdom, a good education, and a good life. I make no claim to having any of these, but do hope that by writing articles on various subjects related to these I may learn something of each of them and make it apparent to anyone reading.

Classics, Mathematics, Philosophy, and Theology are the primary subjects of most of the articles I will write, although anything Computer Science related and any of the Liberal Arts are potential subjects. I plan to discuss my own translations of ancient texts in Latin and Greek, both in terms of their style to see their beauty, and their content & implications to consider the underlying thought; and, although I have chosen not to allow comments for the time being, I heartily welcome discussion on Twitter if you enjoy something I have written.

In case you're curious who the author is behind the articles, I'm a graduate of the University of Texas at Austin in the class of 2017, with a Bachelors of Science & Arts in Mathematics, and a minor in Chinese language (Here's a link to my Resume). My interests are hopefully already apparent; nevertheless, when I'm not busy reading Greek or Latin, I'm usually either working on a coding project (about which I will occasionally write), or giving my mind some time to relax by reading poetry, other literature, or perhaps enjoying a good video game (The Elder Scrolls series is particularly good). I make it no secret that I am Presbyterian, specifically a member of the OPC, but in writing on such varied topics I will almost never be writing dogmatically — my goal in writing is to learn and understand, not to be one more website that only pumps out its own viewpoint. All of that being said, I hope you enjoy something I write, and if you do, let me know!
